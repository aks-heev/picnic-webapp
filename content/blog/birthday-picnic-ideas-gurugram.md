# A Private Birthday Picnic in Gurugram, Planned Down to the Candles

You show up. The rug is laid out, the cushions are arranged, the fairy lights are already on, the cake is waiting, and the person whose birthday it is thinks you did all of this yourself.

That's the whole idea behind a private birthday picnic — and it's why more people in Gurugram are skipping the loud restaurant table for one. You get a reserved corner of a beautiful place, decorated for your person, for your group only. No strangers at the next table singing over your moment. No "we close at 11."

Here's how to plan one, what it includes, and which spots in Gurugram actually work for it.

## Why a picnic beats a restaurant birthday

A restaurant gives you a table. A picnic setup gives you a *setting* — and the difference shows up in every photo.

Three things you get that a dinner reservation can't offer:

**Privacy.** The setup is yours. Whether it's a lawn against the Aravallis or a cozy apartment in DLF Phase 2, nobody else is in the frame.

**The reveal.** Birthdays live and die on the moment the person walks in. A decorated private setup — flowers, candles, balloons, their name — lands in a way a booked table never will.

**Time.** You're not being turned over for the next seating. You settle in, cut the cake when you feel like it, play the playlist you brought.

## What's actually included

Every setup starts with the picnic foundation: the styled seating, rugs, cushions, table setting, and decor, arranged before you arrive. From there, you shape it around the birthday person with add-ons:

- **Cake** — ordered and waiting, so you're not carrying a box through traffic
- **Extra flowers and candles** — for a fuller, warmer setup
- **Bouquet** — handed over at the right moment
- **Photographer** — an hour of candids, so the person who planned everything is actually *in* the pictures
- **Photo printouts** — 10 printed photos to take home the same day
- **Cold pyros** — the sparkler-fountain moment for the cake cutting
- **Sip & Paint** — a session for groups that want something to do together
- **Bonfire or barbeque** — at the outdoor venues, for evening birthdays
- **Movie screening or live music** — for turning a picnic into a full evening (subject to venue confirmation)

You pick these while booking — takes about two minutes — and everything is set up before you walk in.

## Making it feel like *their* birthday, not a template

The setups are styled, but the moments are yours to design. A few combinations we see work well:

**The surprise reveal:** book the slot, tell them it's "just dinner," and walk them into a fully decorated setup with the bouquet handoff timed to the entrance.

**The golden-hour birthday:** an evening slot at Beige Cafe, photographer for the first hour while the light is good, cake at sunset, cold pyros as it gets dark.

**The all-nighter (without the club):** Countryside Offgrid, barbeque, bonfire, and a movie screening once the sky goes dark.

**The tiny, perfect one:** TerraCottage Ochre for two — decorated room, cake, candles, and no one else's schedule.

### What is included in a birthday picnic setup?

The base setup covers styled seating, rugs, cushions, and decor, arranged before you arrive. Cake, flowers, a photographer, photo printouts, cold pyros, and experiences like bonfires or movie screenings can be added while booking.

### Can we do a birthday picnic indoors during summer or monsoon?

Yes — the TerraCottage apartments in DLF Phase 2 are designed exactly for this: fully private indoor picnic setups that don't depend on the weather.

### How far in advance should I book a birthday picnic?

Each venue hosts a limited number of private setups per day, so availability comes down to your specific date — the safest move is booking as soon as the date is fixed.
